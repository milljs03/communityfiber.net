Acceptable Use Policy

Last Updated: January 27, 2026

This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) outlines the acceptable use of Community Fiber's network. All subscribers must comply with this policy to ensure the security and reliability of the network for all users.

1. Illegal Activity

The Service may not be used for any unlawful purpose, including but not limited to:

  • Transmitting child pornography or other illegal content.
  • Copyright infringement (piracy).
  • Fraudulent schemes or phishing.

2. Network Security

Users may not attempt to violate the security of the Community Fiber network or any other network. Prohibited activities include:

  • Hacking, port scanning, or unauthorized access to other systems.
  • Distributing viruses, malware, or worms.
  • Denial of Service (DoS) attacks.

3. Spam and Abuse

The Service may not be used to send unsolicited bulk email (spam). Harassment, threats, or abusive behavior towards other users or Community Fiber staff is strictly prohibited.

4. Excessive Use

While we do not have data caps, we reserve the right to manage the network to ensure fair access for all customers. Using the residential service for high-volume commercial server hosting that degrades the network for neighbors is prohibited.

5. Violations

Community Fiber reserves the right to suspend or terminate service for violations of this AUP.
